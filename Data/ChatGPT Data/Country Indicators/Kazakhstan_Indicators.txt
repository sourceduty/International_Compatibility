
Political Factors (P)

1. International agreements participation: Kazakhstan is actively engaged in numerous international agreements. It participates in the Shanghai Cooperation Organization (SCO), the Collective Security Treaty Organization (CSTO), and the Conference on Interaction and Confidence-Building Measures in Asia (CICA). Additionally, Kazakhstan has signed significant green energy agreements and participates in various global and regional security and trade pacts.

2. Political stability index: Kazakhstan enjoys a moderate level of political stability. The country has undertaken various reforms to enhance governance and maintain stability despite regional and internal challenges.

3. Corruption perception index: According to Transparency International, Kazakhstan scored 37 out of 100 on the 2022 Corruption Perceptions Index, indicating a relatively high level of perceived corruption.

Economic Factors (E)

1. GDP per capita: Kazakhstan's GDP per capita was approximately $10,810 USD in 2022. The economy is driven by oil, gas, and mining industries, along with efforts to diversify into technology and green energy sectors.

2. Trade openness index: Kazakhstan has a high trade openness index due to its significant involvement in international trade agreements and its strategic location for trade between Europe and Asia. The country benefits from numerous bilateral and multilateral trade agreements.

3. Foreign direct investment (FDI) inflows: Kazakhstan attracted about $24.3 billion in FDI in 2022, with substantial investments in the energy, infrastructure, and technology sectors. The government continues to implement reforms to improve the investment climate.

Social Factors (S)

1. Education index: Kazakhstan has made significant investments in education, achieving high literacy rates and continually improving its higher education system. The country is committed to enhancing educational outcomes to support economic development.

2. Healthcare quality index: Kazakhstan's healthcare system is developing, with ongoing reforms aimed at improving access and quality. The country has made strides in expanding healthcare services, though challenges remain.

3. Human development index (HDI): Kazakhstan's HDI is 0.825, placing it in the high human development category. This reflects substantial progress in health, education, and living standards.

Cultural Factors (C)

1. Cultural proximity index (e.g., Hofstede's cultural dimensions): Kazakhstan scores high on collectivism and uncertainty avoidance, indicating a culture that values group harmony and stability. These cultural dimensions significantly influence social and business interactions.

2. Language similarity index: Kazakh is the official language, with Russian widely spoken and used in business and government. This bilingual environment facilitates communication both within the country and internationally.

3. Cross-cultural communication adaptability: Kazakhstan's diverse cultural heritage and strategic location enhance its adaptability in cross-cultural communications. The country has significant experience in international cooperation and trade.

Environmental Factors (Env)

1. Environmental performance index: Kazakhstan scored 44.7 on the Environmental Performance Index, indicating ongoing efforts and challenges in environmental management and sustainability.

2. Carbon footprint per capita: Kazakhstan’s carbon footprint per capita is approximately 15.2 metric tons of CO2, reflecting its heavy reliance on fossil fuels for energy production.

3. Renewable energy usage percentage: Renewable energy accounts for about 3% of Kazakhstan’s total energy consumption. The country is investing in solar, wind, and hydroelectric projects to increase this share.
