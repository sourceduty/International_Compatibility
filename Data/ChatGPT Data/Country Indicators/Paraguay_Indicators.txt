
Political Factors (P)

- International agreements participation:
  Paraguay is active in numerous international agreements, including trade agreements with Brazil and significant participation in global environmental and tax cooperation treaties.

- Political stability index:
  Paraguay experiences moderate political stability, with recurring social protests impacting its stability score negatively.

- Corruption perception index:
  Paraguay ranked 137th out of 180 countries in Transparency International's Corruption Perception Index 2022, indicating significant corruption issues.

Economic Factors (E)

- GDP per capita:
  Paraguay's GDP per capita is approximately $5,820 (World Bank, 2022).

- Trade openness index:
  Paraguay has a high trade openness index due to its significant exports in agriculture, livestock, and hydropower, making it vulnerable to external shocks.

- Foreign direct investment (FDI) inflows:
  In 2022, Paraguay attracted about $589 million in FDI, reflecting a steady interest in its growing economy (World Bank).

Social Factors (S)

- Education index:
  Paraguay has an Education Index score of 0.631, indicating moderate educational attainment levels (UNDP).

- Healthcare quality index:
  The healthcare system in Paraguay scores 56.3 on the Healthcare Access and Quality Index, showing room for improvement.

- Human development index (HDI):
  Paraguay ranks 98th globally with an HDI of 0.724, indicating medium human development levels (UNDP, 2022).

Cultural Factors (C)

- Cultural proximity index:
  Paraguay’s cultural dimensions indicate high collectivism and power distance, similar to other Latin American countries (Hofstede Insights).

- Language similarity index:
  Spanish and Guarani are the official languages of Paraguay, enhancing communication within the region.

- Cross-cultural communication adaptability:
  Paraguayans exhibit high adaptability in cross-cultural communication, influenced by their bilingual and bicultural environment.

Environmental Factors (Env)

- Environmental performance index:
  Paraguay scores 47.6 in the Environmental Performance Index (EPI) 2022, reflecting challenges in environmental sustainability.

- Carbon footprint per capita:
  Paraguay’s carbon footprint per capita is relatively low at approximately 1.6 metric tons of CO2 (World Bank, 2021).

- Renewable energy usage percentage:
  About 90% of Paraguay's energy consumption comes from renewable sources, primarily hydropower, underscoring its commitment to sustainable energy practices (IRENA, 2022).
