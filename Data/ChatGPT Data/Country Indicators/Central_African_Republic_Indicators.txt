
Political Factors (P)

International Agreements Participation
- The Central African Republic is engaged in numerous international agreements, including the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) and various peace agreements aimed at stabilizing the region. Significant efforts include the Political Agreement for Peace and Reconciliation signed in 2019 with 14 armed groups, which aims to restore state authority and address the needs of the population.

Political Stability Index
- CAR has a low political stability index due to ongoing conflicts and political unrest. Internal conflicts, particularly involving armed groups, have resulted in significant instability and humanitarian crises.

Corruption Perception Index
- Transparency International ranks CAR low on the Corruption Perception Index, indicating high levels of corruption. The country faces significant governance challenges, impacting its political and economic environment.

Economic Factors (E)

GDP per Capita
- As of 2023, CAR’s GDP per capita is approximately $511. The economy relies heavily on agriculture, mining, and forestry, but is hindered by conflict and limited infrastructure.

Trade Openness Index
- CAR has a moderate trade openness index, engaging in trade primarily with neighboring countries and international partners. Efforts are ongoing to improve trade policies and integrate more deeply into global markets.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows
- CAR attracts limited foreign direct investment due to its unstable political situation and weak infrastructure. Efforts to improve the investment climate are ongoing, but challenges remain significant.

Social Factors (S)

Education Index
- CAR faces severe challenges in education, with low literacy rates and limited access to quality education. International efforts are focused on improving educational infrastructure and training teachers to enhance educational outcomes.

Healthcare Quality Index
- The healthcare system in CAR is underdeveloped, with significant gaps in access to healthcare services, especially in rural areas. The country struggles with high rates of maternal and infant mortality, and ongoing conflicts exacerbate healthcare challenges.

Human Development Index (HDI)
- CAR ranks very low on the Human Development Index, reflecting severe challenges in health, education, and income levels. The country remains one of the least developed globally, with significant efforts needed to improve living standards.

Cultural Factors (C)

Cultural Proximity Index
- CAR's cultural dimensions reflect high power distance and collectivism, influenced by its diverse ethnic composition and traditional societal structures. These cultural traits impact social interactions and organizational behavior within the country.

Language Similarity Index
- The official languages of CAR are French and Sango, which facilitates communication and cultural exchange within the region and with international partners. This bilingual nature supports cross-cultural adaptability.

Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability
- CAR’s multicultural society and emphasis on bilingual education contribute to adaptability in cross-cultural communication. The population’s exposure to diverse cultures supports effective international interactions.

Environmental Factors (Env)

Environmental Performance Index
- CAR faces significant environmental challenges, including deforestation and biodiversity loss. Efforts are being made to promote sustainable management of natural resources and enhance environmental conservation.

Carbon Footprint per Capita
- CAR’s carbon footprint per capita is low, reflecting its developing economy and limited industrial activities. Efforts to increase the use of renewable energy and reduce emissions are ongoing.

Renewable Energy Usage Percentage
- The usage of renewable energy in CAR is growing, with investments in hydropower and solar energy projects. These initiatives aim to diversify the energy mix and promote sustainability.
