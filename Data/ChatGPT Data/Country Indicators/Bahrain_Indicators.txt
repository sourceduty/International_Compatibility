
Political Factors (P)

International Agreements Participation
- Bahrain is actively involved in numerous international agreements, including the Comprehensive Security Integration and Prosperity Agreement with the United States, which enhances cooperation across defense, security, technology, trade, and investment. The country is also part of various bilateral investment treaties, particularly with European and Middle Eastern countries, to promote foreign investment and economic cooperation.

Political Stability Index
- Bahrain has maintained relative political stability despite regional tensions and domestic challenges. The government continues to address internal demands for political and social reforms while maintaining its strategic alliances and economic partnerships.

Economic Factors (E)

GDP per Capita
- As of 2023, Bahrain's GDP per capita is approximately $24,000. The economy is diversified, with significant contributions from the financial services, manufacturing, and oil sectors.

Trade Openness Index
- Bahrain has a high trade openness index, supported by its free trade agreements with the United States and other major trading partners. The establishment of the US Trade Zone in Bahrain is expected to further enhance trade and economic growth.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows
- Bahrain continues to attract substantial foreign direct investment, particularly in financial services, real estate, and manufacturing. In 2022, FDI inflows reached approximately $1.95 billion, reflecting the country's favorable investment climate and regulatory environment.

Social Factors (S)

Education Index
- The education system in Bahrain is well-developed, with a high literacy rate and significant government investment in both primary and tertiary education. The country emphasizes quality education to support its economic and social development.

Healthcare Quality Index
- Bahrain's healthcare system provides comprehensive and high-quality medical services. The government continues to invest in healthcare infrastructure and services, ensuring accessible and effective care for all citizens.

Human Development Index (HDI)
- Bahrain ranks high on the Human Development Index, reflecting strong performance in health, education, and income levels. Ongoing investments in social services are crucial to maintaining and improving this ranking.

Cultural Factors (C)

Cultural Proximity Index
- Bahrain's cultural dimensions, influenced by its historical and regional context, reflect a society with moderate power distance and collectivism. These cultural traits influence social interactions and business practices within the country.

Language Similarity Index
- The official language is Arabic, with English widely spoken, especially in business and government. This linguistic capability facilitates international business and cultural exchanges.

Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability
- Bahrain's multicultural society and strategic location as a trade hub contribute to strong adaptability in cross-cultural communication. The population's exposure to diverse cultures enhances effective international interactions.

Environmental Factors (Env)

Environmental Performance Index
- Bahrain faces significant environmental challenges, including air pollution and water scarcity. The government is working on improving environmental sustainability through various initiatives, such as increasing green spaces and promoting sustainable practices.

Carbon Footprint per Capita
- Bahrain's carbon footprint per capita is relatively high, primarily due to its reliance on fossil fuels. However, efforts are being made to increase the use of renewable energy sources and reduce emissions.

Renewable Energy Usage Percentage
- The usage of renewable energy in Bahrain is increasing, with significant investments in solar and wind energy. These initiatives aim to diversify the energy mix and promote sustainability.
