
Political Factors (P)

1. International agreements participation: India has signed multiple international agreements, including 14 Free Trade Agreements (FTAs) with various countries, including the European Free Trade Association (EFTA). India has also been a significant player in international climate negotiations, contributing its Intended Nationally Determined Contribution (INDC) under the Paris Agreement to reduce emissions intensity and increase non-fossil fuel energy capacity.

2. Political stability index: India faces challenges in political stability due to regional conflicts, social unrest, and political disputes. The overall political environment remains complex, impacting governance and policy implementation.

3. Corruption perception index: According to Transparency International, India scored 40 out of 100 in the 2022 Corruption Perceptions Index, indicating moderate levels of perceived corruption.

Economic Factors (E)

1. GDP per capita: India's GDP per capita was approximately $2,277 USD in 2022. The economy continues to grow, driven by sectors such as services, manufacturing, and information technology.

2. Trade openness index: India's trade openness index reflects significant engagement in international trade, with ongoing negotiations for new trade agreements to boost exports and market access.

3. Foreign direct investment (FDI) inflows: India attracted around $60 billion in FDI in 2022, with significant investments in sectors like technology, pharmaceuticals, and manufacturing.

Social Factors (S)

1. Education index: India has made substantial progress in education, with increasing literacy rates and expanding access to education. The country's education index continues to improve due to government initiatives and investments.

2. Healthcare quality index: India's healthcare system faces challenges, but there have been improvements in healthcare quality and access, driven by both public and private sector efforts.

3. Human development index (HDI): India's HDI is 0.645, placing it in the medium human development category, reflecting advancements in health, education, and living standards.

Cultural Factors (C)

1. Cultural proximity index (e.g., Hofstede's cultural dimensions): India scores high on power distance and collectivism, indicating a hierarchical society with strong community ties. These cultural dimensions influence social and business interactions.

2. Language similarity index: Hindi and English are the official languages, with English widely used in business and education. India's multilingualism enhances its global communication adaptability.

3. Cross-cultural communication adaptability: India's diverse cultural heritage and extensive diaspora contribute to its high adaptability in cross-cultural communication.

Environmental Factors (Env)

1. Environmental performance index: India scored 27.6 on the Environmental Performance Index, highlighting significant environmental challenges, including air and water pollution.

2. Carbon footprint per capita: India's carbon footprint per capita is about 1.9 metric tons of CO2, reflecting its developing economy and large population.

3. Renewable energy usage percentage: Renewable energy accounts for about 38% of India's total installed power capacity, with significant investments in solar, wind, and hydropower projects.
