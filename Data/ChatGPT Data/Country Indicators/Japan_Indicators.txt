
Political Factors (P)

1. International agreements participation: Japan is actively involved in numerous international agreements. It has signed agreements such as the U.S.-Japan Trade Agreement, the Japan-EU Economic Partnership Agreement, and is a member of the Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership (CPTPP). Japan also continues to enhance its bilateral cooperation with the United States on security, technology, and climate change initiatives.

2. Political stability index: Japan enjoys a high degree of political stability, underpinned by its democratic institutions and stable governance. However, the country faces challenges related to regional security and domestic political dynamics.

3. Corruption perception index: According to Transparency International, Japan scored 73 out of 100 on the 2022 Corruption Perceptions Index, indicating relatively low levels of perceived corruption.

Economic Factors (E)

1. GDP per capita: Japan's GDP per capita was approximately $39,285 USD in 2022, reflecting its status as a high-income economy driven by advanced manufacturing, technology, and services sectors.

2. Trade openness index: Japan has a high trade openness index, engaging in extensive international trade. It benefits from numerous free trade agreements, enhancing its global economic integration.

3. Foreign direct investment (FDI) inflows: Japan attracted around $24.5 billion in FDI in 2022. The country's innovation-driven economy and strong legal frameworks make it an attractive destination for foreign investors.

Social Factors (S)

1. Education index: Japan places a strong emphasis on education, with high literacy rates and significant investments in higher education. The country's education system is globally recognized for its quality and rigor.

2. Healthcare quality index: Japan's healthcare system is highly developed, characterized by excellent healthcare outcomes and a life expectancy of 84 years. The country spends approximately 10% of its GDP on healthcare.

3. Human development index (HDI): Japan's HDI is 0.919, placing it in the very high human development category, reflecting significant achievements in health, education, and living standards.

Cultural Factors (C)

1. Cultural proximity index (e.g., Hofstede's cultural dimensions): Japan scores high on collectivism and uncertainty avoidance, indicating a culture that values group harmony and stability. These cultural dimensions significantly influence social and business interactions.

2. Language similarity index: Japanese is the official language, with English widely taught and used in business and academia. This multilingual environment facilitates international communication.

3. Cross-cultural communication adaptability: Japan's rich cultural heritage and extensive international engagements enhance its adaptability in cross-cultural communications. The country's significant diaspora also contributes to its global cultural connections.

Environmental Factors (Env)

1. Environmental performance index: Japan scored 63.9 on the Environmental Performance Index, reflecting strong environmental policies and ongoing efforts to improve sustainability and environmental protection.

2. Carbon footprint per capita: Japan’s carbon footprint per capita is approximately 8.9 metric tons of CO2, reflecting its industrial activities and energy consumption patterns.

3. Renewable energy usage percentage: Renewable energy accounts for about 20% of Japan’s total energy consumption, with significant investments in solar, wind, and geothermal energy projects to increase this share.
