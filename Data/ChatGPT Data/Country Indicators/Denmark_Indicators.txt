
Political Factors (P)

1. International Agreements Participation:
   Denmark is an active participant in numerous international agreements, including the Paris Agreement, the Kyoto Protocol, and various EU agreements due to its membership in the European Union. It is also a member of the World Trade Organization (WTO) and has bilateral investment treaties with several countries.

2. Political Stability Index:
   According to the World Bank, Denmark has a high political stability index, reflecting its strong democratic institutions and governance.

3. Corruption Perception Index:
   Transparency International ranks Denmark among the least corrupt countries in the world, placing it 1st out of 180 countries in the Corruption Perceptions Index, indicating very low levels of perceived corruption.

Economic Factors (E)

1. GDP per Capita:
   Denmark's GDP per capita is approximately $60,000 USD, indicating a high standard of living and a well-developed economy.

2. Trade Openness Index:
   Denmark has a high trade openness index, with significant exports and imports contributing to its economy. Major trading partners include Germany, Sweden, and the Netherlands.

3. Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows:
   Denmark attracts substantial FDI, particularly in sectors like pharmaceuticals, maritime shipping, and renewable energy. Recent data shows significant FDI inflows annually.

Social Factors (S)

1. Education Index:
   Denmark scores highly on the education index, with a strong emphasis on free education for all levels, leading to high literacy rates and strong educational outcomes.

2. Healthcare Quality Index:
   The healthcare system in Denmark is rated highly, with universal healthcare providing comprehensive services to its citizens. The quality of healthcare is among the best globally.

3. Human Development Index (HDI):
   Denmark has a very high HDI score of 0.940, reflecting excellent performance in life expectancy, education, and income per capita.

Cultural Factors (C)

1. Cultural Proximity Index:
   According to Hofstede's cultural dimensions, Denmark scores low on power distance and high on individualism, indicating a culture that values equality and individual rights.

2. Language Similarity Index:
   The official language is Danish, which shares similarities with other Scandinavian languages, facilitating communication within the Nordic region.

3. Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability:
   Denmark shows strong adaptability in cross-cultural communication, driven by its openness to international business and diverse expatriate community.

Environmental Factors (Env)

1. Environmental Performance Index:
   Denmark is a global leader in environmental performance, consistently ranking high on the Environmental Performance Index due to its strong policies on air quality, renewable energy, and sustainable practices.

2. Carbon Footprint per Capita:
   Denmark's carbon footprint per capita is moderate, with ongoing efforts to reduce emissions through extensive use of renewable energy sources.

3. Renewable Energy Usage Percentage:
   Denmark is a pioneer in renewable energy, with over 50% of its electricity generated from wind and other renewable sources.
