
Political Factors (P)

International Agreements Participation
- Botswana is actively involved in numerous international agreements. It is a member of the Southern African Customs Union (SACU), which allows duty-free trade among member states. Additionally, Botswana participates in the African Continental Free Trade Agreement (AfCFTA) and the Southern African Development Community (SADC) Free Trade Protocol, promoting regional economic integration and trade.

Political Stability Index
- Botswana is known for its political stability, which is one of the highest in Africa. The country's democratic governance and peaceful elections contribute to a stable political environment, though occasional challenges such as corruption and socio-economic issues persist.

Corruption Perception Index
- Botswana is ranked relatively well by Transparency International, indicating lower levels of corruption compared to many other African nations. Efforts to combat corruption are ongoing, with various legal frameworks and institutions in place to enhance transparency.

Economic Factors (E)

GDP per Capita
- As of 2023, Botswana's GDP per capita is approximately $8,200. The economy is primarily driven by mining (especially diamonds), tourism, and financial services.

Trade Openness Index
- Botswana has a high trade openness index due to its participation in regional trade agreements and efforts to diversify its trade partners. The country exports diamonds, copper, nickel, and beef, among other products.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows
- Botswana continues to attract FDI, particularly in the mining sector. The government encourages foreign investment through various incentives and a stable regulatory environment.

Social Factors (S)

Education Index
- Botswana has made significant progress in education, with high literacy rates and substantial government investment in primary, secondary, and tertiary education. The focus is on improving quality and accessibility across the country.

Healthcare Quality Index
- The healthcare system in Botswana is well-developed, offering comprehensive medical services. However, challenges remain in rural areas where access to healthcare is limited. The government is working to improve healthcare infrastructure and services.

Human Development Index (HDI)
- Botswana ranks moderately on the Human Development Index, reflecting progress in health, education, and living standards. Continued investments in social services are necessary to improve these indicators further.

Cultural Factors (C)

Cultural Proximity Index
- Botswana's cultural dimensions, influenced by its diverse ethnic groups and historical context, reflect moderate individualism and a high power distance. These cultural traits impact social interactions and organizational behavior within the country.

Language Similarity Index
- The official languages are English and Setswana, facilitating communication and cultural exchange within the region. This linguistic capability enhances international cooperation and business relationships.

Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability
- Botswana's multicultural society and robust education system contribute to strong adaptability in cross-cultural communication. The population's exposure to diverse cultures supports effective international interactions.

Environmental Factors (Env)

Environmental Performance Index
- Botswana faces significant environmental challenges, including managing water scarcity and preserving biodiversity. The government implements policies to improve environmental sustainability and reduce emissions.

Carbon Footprint per Capita
- Botswana's carbon footprint per capita is relatively high, largely due to its reliance on coal for energy. Efforts are being made to increase the use of renewable energy sources and improve energy efficiency.

Renewable Energy Usage Percentage
- The usage of renewable energy in Botswana is increasing, with investments in solar and wind energy projects. These initiatives aim to diversify the energy mix and promote sustainability.
