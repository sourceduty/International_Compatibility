
Political Factors (P)

1. International agreements participation: Jordan is actively involved in numerous international agreements. It is a member of the United Nations and several of its specialized agencies, such as the Food and Agriculture Organization and the World Health Organization. Jordan is also a founding member of the Arab League and the Organisation of Islamic Cooperation, and participates in various trade agreements including the U.S.-Jordan Free Trade Agreement and regional agreements within CARICOM.

2. Political stability index: Jordan experiences moderate political stability. While it maintains a relatively stable government, it faces challenges related to regional conflicts, economic pressures, and social unrest. Nonetheless, the country is known for its resilience and adaptive policies.

3. Corruption perception index: According to Transparency International, Jordan scored 49 out of 100 on the 2022 Corruption Perceptions Index, indicating moderate levels of perceived corruption.

Economic Factors (E)

1. GDP per capita: Jordan's GDP per capita was approximately $4,500 USD in 2022. The economy is diversified but faces significant challenges due to regional instability and limited natural resources.

2. Trade openness index: Jordan has a high trade openness index, engaging extensively in international trade through numerous agreements and partnerships. The country benefits from trade agreements with the United States, European Union, and regional partners.

3. Foreign direct investment (FDI) inflows: Jordan attracted around $950 million in FDI in 2022, with investments focusing on sectors such as energy, manufacturing, and services. The government continues to implement reforms to enhance the investment climate.

Social Factors (S)

1. Education index: Jordan places a strong emphasis on education, achieving high literacy rates and significant investments in higher education. Despite economic challenges, the country continues to prioritize educational development.

2. Healthcare quality index: Jordan's healthcare system is relatively developed, providing a range of services with a focus on improving quality and access. The country is working on reforms to enhance healthcare infrastructure and services.

3. Human development index (HDI): Jordan's HDI is 0.735, placing it in the medium human development category. This reflects steady progress in health, education, and living standards.

Cultural Factors (C)

1. Cultural proximity index (e.g., Hofstede's cultural dimensions): Jordan scores high on collectivism and uncertainty avoidance, indicating a culture that values group harmony and stability. These cultural traits influence social interactions and business practices.

2. Language similarity index: Arabic is the official language of Jordan, with English widely taught and used in business and academia. This bilingual environment facilitates international communication.

3. Cross-cultural communication adaptability: Jordan's diverse cultural heritage and historical trade links enhance its adaptability in cross-cultural communications. The country's experience with international cooperation and a significant diaspora further contribute to its global cultural connections.

Environmental Factors (Env)

1. Environmental performance index: Jordan scored 53.2 on the Environmental Performance Index, reflecting efforts and challenges in environmental management and sustainability. The country is focusing on initiatives to improve its environmental performance.

2. Carbon footprint per capita: Jordan’s carbon footprint per capita is approximately 3.5 metric tons of CO2, reflecting its energy consumption patterns and industrial activities.

3. Renewable energy usage percentage: Renewable energy accounts for about 13% of Jordan’s total energy consumption, with significant investments in solar and wind energy projects to increase this share.
