
Political Factors (P)

- International agreements participation:
  Peru participates in numerous international agreements, including the Pacific Alliance, various United Nations treaties, and regional environmental accords.

- Political stability index:
  Peru has a political stability index of -1.06, indicating moderate instability due to political turmoil and social unrest (World Bank, 2022).

- Corruption perception index:
  Peru ranked 105th out of 180 countries in Transparency International's Corruption Perception Index 2022, reflecting ongoing corruption challenges.

Economic Factors (E)

- GDP per capita:
  Peru's GDP per capita is approximately $6,807 (World Bank, 2022).

- Trade openness index:
  Peru has a high trade openness index due to its significant exports in mining, agriculture, and fisheries, making it vulnerable to global market fluctuations.

- Foreign direct investment (FDI) inflows:
  In 2022, Peru attracted about $8.2 billion in FDI, reflecting strong investment interest in its mining and energy sectors (World Bank).

Social Factors (S)

- Education index:
  Peru has an Education Index score of 0.650, indicating moderate educational attainment levels (UNDP).

- Healthcare quality index:
  The healthcare system in Peru scores 59.2 on the Healthcare Access and Quality Index, indicating room for improvement in healthcare services.

- Human development index (HDI):
  Peru ranks 79th globally with an HDI of 0.777, indicating high human development levels (UNDP, 2022).

Cultural Factors (C)

- Cultural proximity index:
  Peru’s cultural dimensions indicate high collectivism and power distance, similar to other Latin American countries (Hofstede Insights).

- Language similarity index:
  Spanish is the official language of Peru, with significant indigenous languages spoken, enhancing cultural diversity and communication.

- Cross-cultural communication adaptability:
  Peruvians exhibit high adaptability in cross-cultural communication, influenced by their multicultural and multilingual environment.

Environmental Factors (Env)

- Environmental performance index:
  Peru scores 39.9 in the Environmental Performance Index (EPI) 2022, reflecting significant challenges in environmental sustainability.

- Carbon footprint per capita:
  Peru’s carbon footprint per capita is relatively low at approximately 1.8 metric tons of CO2 (World Bank, 2021).

- Renewable energy usage percentage:
  About 59% of Peru's energy consumption comes from renewable sources, primarily hydropower, indicating a strong commitment to sustainable energy practices (IRENA, 2022).
