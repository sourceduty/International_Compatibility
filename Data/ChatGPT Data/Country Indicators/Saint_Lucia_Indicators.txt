
Political Factors (P)

- International Agreements Participation:
  Saint Lucia is actively involved in numerous international agreements, including the United Nations, the Commonwealth, the Organization of American States (OAS), and the Caribbean Community (CARICOM). Recently, Saint Lucia signed agreements with Venezuela to enhance cooperation in areas such as development, education, tourism, and air transportation.

- Political Stability Index:
  Saint Lucia enjoys a relatively stable political environment, supported by comprehensive governance strategies and initiatives to improve transparency.

- Corruption Perception Index:
  Efforts are ongoing in Saint Lucia to improve governance and reduce corruption. While specific recent rankings are not detailed, initiatives to enhance integrity in public life are evident.

Economic Factors (E)

- GDP per Capita:
  As of 2022, Saint Lucia's GDP per capita is approximately $10,260 (World Bank).

- Trade Openness Index:
  Saint Lucia has a high trade openness index, reflecting significant import and export activities facilitated by its membership in regional trade blocs like CARICOM.

- Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows:
  In 2022, Saint Lucia attracted significant FDI, particularly in tourism and health tourism, with investments totaling over $100 million.

Social Factors (S)

- Education Index:
  Saint Lucia has made significant strides in education, offering free tuition at the community college level and reducing university costs. The education index reflects these improvements, showing moderate to high educational attainment.

- Healthcare Quality Index:
  The healthcare system in Saint Lucia is improving, with a focus on national health insurance and climate-smart infrastructure, resulting in a moderate healthcare quality index.

- Human Development Index (HDI):
  Saint Lucia has a high human development index, with a score of 0.759 in 2022, reflecting advancements in health, education, and income (UNDP).

Cultural Factors (C)

- Cultural Proximity Index:
  Saint Lucia exhibits cultural traits common to the Caribbean, with high collectivism and power distance, indicating strong community ties and hierarchical structures.

- Language Similarity Index:
  English is the official language, which facilitates communication within the region and with global partners.

- Cross-cultural Communication Adaptability:
  The population shows high adaptability in cross-cultural communication, influenced by diverse cultural interactions within the Caribbean and international diplomatic engagements.

Environmental Factors (Env)

- Environmental Performance Index:
  Saint Lucia scores moderately on the Environmental Performance Index, with efforts focused on climate change mitigation and environmental sustainability.

- Carbon Footprint per Capita:
  The carbon footprint per capita is relatively low at approximately 2.0 metric tons of CO2, reflecting the small industrial base and reliance on renewable energy sources (World Bank).

- Renewable Energy Usage Percentage:
  Around 30% of the country's energy consumption comes from renewable sources, primarily solar and wind energy, highlighting a commitment to sustainable energy practices.
