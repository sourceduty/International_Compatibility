
Political Factors (P)

International Agreements Participation
- Antigua and Barbuda actively participates in numerous international agreements and organizations. It is a member of the United Nations, the Commonwealth of Nations, the Organization of American States, the Organisation of Eastern Caribbean States (OECS), and the Eastern Caribbean Currency Union (ECCU). The country has also signed bilateral investment treaties with several countries, including Denmark, Norway, Sweden, and Switzerland.

Political Stability Index
- Antigua and Barbuda enjoys a stable political environment, largely due to its democratic governance and regular elections. The political landscape is characterized by relatively low levels of political violence and unrest, contributing to its overall stability.

Corruption Perception Index
- Corruption remains a concern in Antigua and Barbuda, although efforts are being made to address it. According to Transparency International, the country faces challenges in governance and transparency, which impact its corruption perception index.

Economic Factors (E)

GDP per Capita
- As of 2023, Antigua and Barbuda's GDP per capita is approximately $16,800. The economy is primarily driven by tourism, financial services, and construction, with tourism being the most significant contributor to GDP.

Trade Openness Index
- Antigua and Barbuda has a high trade openness index, facilitated by its membership in regional trade agreements such as the OECS and its active participation in international trade. The country benefits from preferential trade agreements with major markets, enhancing its trade capabilities.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows
- The country continues to attract foreign direct investment, particularly in the tourism and real estate sectors. Recent initiatives and policy reforms have aimed to create a more favorable investment climate to boost FDI inflows.

Social Factors (S)

Education Index
- The education system in Antigua and Barbuda is well-developed, with a high literacy rate and significant government investment in educational infrastructure. The country emphasizes both primary and secondary education, and higher education opportunities are available locally and through international partnerships.

Healthcare Quality Index
- Healthcare in Antigua and Barbuda is improving, with ongoing investments in healthcare infrastructure and services. The country has made strides in providing comprehensive healthcare to its citizens, although challenges remain in terms of access and quality.

Human Development Index (HDI)
- Antigua and Barbuda ranks high on the Human Development Index, reflecting strong performance in health, education, and standard of living. The country's continued focus on social services and economic development supports its high HDI ranking.

Cultural Factors (C)

Cultural Proximity Index
- The cultural dimensions of Antigua and Barbuda, influenced by its colonial history and diverse population, reflect a blend of African, European, and indigenous influences. This cultural mix affects social interactions and organizational behavior within the country.

Language Similarity Index
- The official language of Antigua and Barbuda is English, which facilitates communication and cultural exchange with other English-speaking countries. This linguistic similarity supports the country's adaptability in international relations and business.

Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability
- The diverse cultural heritage of Antigua and Barbuda contributes to a high level of cross-cultural communication adaptability. The population's exposure to various cultures through tourism and international relations enhances their ability to engage effectively across different cultural contexts.

Environmental Factors (Env)

Environmental Performance Index
- Antigua and Barbuda faces environmental challenges such as vulnerability to climate change and natural disasters. The government is actively working on improving its environmental performance through sustainable practices and international cooperation.

Carbon Footprint per Capita
- The carbon footprint per capita in Antigua and Barbuda is moderate, with ongoing efforts to reduce emissions through renewable energy initiatives and environmental policies.

Renewable Energy Usage Percentage
- The country is increasing its use of renewable energy, focusing on solar and wind energy projects. These efforts aim to diversify the energy mix and reduce reliance on fossil fuels, contributing to environmental sustainability.
