
Political Factors (P)

1. International Agreements Participation:
   The Czech Republic, as a member of the European Union (EU), participates in all free trade arrangements entered into by the EU. It is also a member of the World Trade Organization (WTO) and has signed numerous bilateral and multilateral agreements, including the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) and various Bilateral Investment Treaties (BITs).

2. Political Stability Index:
   According to the World Bank's Worldwide Governance Indicators, the Czech Republic has a high level of political stability, reflecting a well-functioning democratic system with stable governance structures.

3. Corruption Perception Index:
   Transparency International's Corruption Perceptions Index ranks the Czech Republic 49th out of 180 countries, indicating moderate levels of perceived corruption.

Economic Factors (E)

1. GDP per Capita:
   As of the latest data, the GDP per capita in the Czech Republic is approximately $25,600 USD, reflecting its status as a high-income economy within the EU.

2. Trade Openness Index:
   The Czech Republic has a high trade openness index due to its integration into the EU single market, which allows for extensive trade with other EU member states and global partners.

3. Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows:
   The country has attracted significant FDI, particularly in sectors like manufacturing, technology, and services. Recent data shows FDI inflows amounting to several billion USD annually.

Social Factors (S)

1. Education Index:
   The Czech Republic scores high on the education index, with a well-developed public education system and high literacy rates. The country is noted for its strong emphasis on science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) education.

2. Healthcare Quality Index:
   The healthcare system in the Czech Republic is of high quality, with comprehensive coverage and services. The country ranks well in terms of healthcare access and outcomes.

3. Human Development Index (HDI):
   The Czech Republic has a high HDI score of 0.900, indicating significant achievements in life expectancy, education, and standard of living.

Cultural Factors (C)

1. Cultural Proximity Index:
   Using Hofstede's cultural dimensions, the Czech Republic shows moderate levels of individualism and uncertainty avoidance, reflecting a society that values both community ties and structured environments.

2. Language Similarity Index:
   The official language is Czech, which is closely related to Slovak, facilitating regional communication within Central Europe.

3. Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability:
   The Czech Republic exhibits strong adaptability in cross-cultural communication, supported by its tourism industry and increasing international business interactions.
