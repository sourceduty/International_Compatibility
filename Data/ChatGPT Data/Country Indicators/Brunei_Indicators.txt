
Political Factors (P)

International Agreements Participation
- Brunei is actively involved in various international agreements. The country is a member of the Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership (CPTPP), which it joined in 2023, enhancing its trade relationships and market access globally. Brunei is also a member of ASEAN, APEC, WTO, and participates in regional initiatives like the Brunei-Indonesia-Malaysia-Philippines East ASEAN Growth Area (BIMP-EAGA).

Political Stability Index
- Brunei maintains a stable political environment under an absolute monarchy. The country is known for its internal political stability, supported by the ruling Sultan's firm control over governance and minimal political unrest.

Corruption Perception Index
- Brunei ranks relatively well on Transparency International's Corruption Perception Index, reflecting low levels of corruption. The country has stringent laws and regulations in place to combat corruption and ensure transparency in governance.

Economic Factors (E)

GDP per Capita
- As of 2023, Brunei's GDP per capita is approximately $32,000. The economy is heavily reliant on oil and gas exports, which constitute the main source of revenue.

Trade Openness Index
- Brunei has a high trade openness index, facilitated by its participation in multiple international trade agreements. The country exports petroleum and natural gas products primarily to Japan, South Korea, and other ASEAN countries.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows
- Brunei continues to attract foreign direct investment, particularly in the energy sector. The government offers various incentives to foreign investors to diversify the economy beyond oil and gas.

Social Factors (S)

Education Index
- Brunei has a high literacy rate and a well-developed education system, with significant government investment in primary, secondary, and higher education. The focus is on improving educational outcomes and accessibility across the country.

Healthcare Quality Index
- The healthcare system in Brunei is comprehensive, providing universal healthcare with high standards of medical care. The government continues to invest in healthcare infrastructure and services to improve health outcomes.

Human Development Index (HDI)
- Brunei ranks high on the Human Development Index, reflecting strong performance in health, education, and income levels. Continued efforts are made to maintain and improve living standards.

Cultural Factors (C)

Cultural Proximity Index
- Brunei's cultural dimensions reflect a society with high power distance and collectivism, influenced by its Islamic heritage and traditional values. These cultural traits significantly impact social interactions and organizational behavior within the country.

Language Similarity Index
- The official language of Brunei is Malay, with English widely used in business and education. This linguistic capability facilitates communication and cultural exchange within the ASEAN region and globally.

Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability
- Brunei’s multicultural society and emphasis on education contribute to strong adaptability in cross-cultural communication. The population’s exposure to diverse cultures supports effective international interactions.

Environmental Factors (Env)

Environmental Performance Index
- Brunei faces environmental challenges, including the need to manage its natural resources sustainably. The government implements policies to improve environmental sustainability and reduce emissions.

Carbon Footprint per Capita
- Brunei’s carbon footprint per capita is relatively high due to its reliance on fossil fuels. Efforts are being made to increase the use of renewable energy sources and enhance environmental conservation.

Renewable Energy Usage Percentage
- The usage of renewable energy in Brunei is growing, with investments in solar and biomass energy projects. These initiatives aim to diversify the energy mix and promote sustainability.
