
Political Factors (P)

International Agreements Participation
- Armenia is a member of several international organizations and agreements, including the Eurasian Economic Union (EAEU) and a Comprehensive and Enhanced Partnership Agreement with the European Union (EU). In 2023, Armenia invited the European Union to deploy a civilian mission to help stabilize conflict-affected areas. However, Armenia has frozen its participation in the Collective Security Treaty Organization (CSTO) due to dissatisfaction with its response to regional security issues.

Political Stability Index
- Armenia has faced significant political instability due to ongoing conflicts with Azerbaijan over Nagorno-Karabakh and domestic political challenges. Despite these issues, the government is working on various reforms to improve governance and stability.

Corruption Perception Index
- Armenia's corruption perception remains a challenge, ranked 63rd out of 180 countries by Transparency International in 2022. The government continues to implement anti-corruption reforms, but enforcement remains inconsistent.

Economic Factors (E)

GDP per Capita
- As of 2023, Armenia's GDP per capita is approximately $4,850. The economy is supported by sectors like energy, mining, and IT, with recent growth driven by an influx of capital and businesses relocating from Russia, Belarus, and Ukraine.

Trade Openness Index
- Armenia is moderately open to trade, benefiting from its membership in the EAEU and bilateral trade agreements. Trade policies aim to integrate Armenia more closely with international markets.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows
- FDI in Armenia is relatively low but growing, especially in energy and mining. The government is working to improve the investment climate, though challenges such as judicial inefficiency and regulatory issues persist.

Social Factors (S)

Education Index
- Armenia has a high literacy rate and a well-established education system, emphasizing STEM fields and higher education. Ongoing reforms aim to improve educational quality and accessibility.

Healthcare Quality Index
- The healthcare system in Armenia is improving, with efforts to enhance healthcare services and infrastructure. However, challenges remain in terms of access and quality of care, particularly in rural areas.

Human Development Index (HDI)
- Armenia ranks high on the Human Development Index, reflecting good performance in health, education, and living standards. Continued reforms and investments in social services are necessary to maintain and improve these outcomes.

Cultural Factors (C)

Cultural Proximity Index
- Armenia's cultural dimensions are influenced by its historical ties with Europe and the Middle East. The society tends to value collectivism and long-term relationships, impacting social interactions and business practices.

Language Similarity Index
- The official language is Armenian, which is unique but also uses an alphabet distinct from its neighbors. This linguistic uniqueness can pose challenges but also fosters a strong national identity.

Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability
- Armenia's diverse cultural heritage and education system contribute to moderate adaptability in cross-cultural communication. Efforts are ongoing to improve language education and cultural exchange programs.

Environmental Factors (Env)

Environmental Performance Index
- Armenia faces environmental challenges such as deforestation, pollution, and water management issues. The government is working on improving environmental sustainability through various national and international initiatives.

Carbon Footprint per Capita
- Armenia's carbon footprint per capita is relatively low, with efforts to increase the use of renewable energy sources and reduce emissions.

Renewable Energy Usage Percentage
- The usage of renewable energy in Armenia is increasing, with investments in hydroelectric and solar power projects aimed at reducing reliance on fossil fuels and improving energy sustainability.
