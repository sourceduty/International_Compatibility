
Political Factors (P)

1. International agreements participation: Honduras is actively involved in numerous international agreements, including those related to trade, environmental protection, and regional cooperation. Key memberships include the United Nations, World Trade Organization (WTO), and the Central American Integration System (SICA).

2. Political stability index: Honduras faces significant political challenges. The country has experienced political instability, with concerns about the consolidation of power by the current administration and the impact on democratic processes and institutions.

3. Corruption perception index: According to Transparency International, Honduras scored 23 out of 100 on the Corruption Perceptions Index in 2022, indicating high levels of perceived corruption.

Economic Factors (E)

1. GDP per capita: In 2022, Honduras' GDP per capita was approximately $6,253 USD. The economy has faced significant challenges due to the COVID-19 pandemic and natural disasters but has shown some recovery.

2. Trade openness index: Honduras' trade openness is significant, with trade (exports and imports) accounting for a considerable portion of its GDP. The country benefits from various trade agreements, including the Central America-Dominican Republic Free Trade Agreement (CAFTA-DR) with the United States.

3. Foreign direct investment (FDI) inflows: FDI inflows in Honduras were around 1.0% of GDP in 2020, reflecting cautious investment interest due to the country’s political and economic instability.

Social Factors (S)

1. Education index: Honduras faces educational challenges, with low literacy rates and limited access to quality education, particularly in rural areas. Efforts are ongoing to improve educational outcomes and infrastructure.

2. Healthcare quality index: Healthcare in Honduras is underfunded and underdeveloped. The healthcare system struggles to meet basic needs, and life expectancy is lower compared to other countries in the region.

3. Human development index (HDI): Honduras' HDI is 0.634, placing it in the medium human development category. This reflects challenges in health, education, and standard of living.

Cultural Factors (C)

1. Cultural proximity index (e.g., Hofstede's cultural dimensions): Honduras scores high on power distance and collectivism, indicating a society that values hierarchy and community ties.

2. Language similarity index: Spanish is the official language of Honduras, widely spoken across the country, facilitating communication within the Latin American region.

3. Cross-cultural communication adaptability: Honduras has a diverse cultural heritage and history of interaction with various international organizations, enhancing its adaptability in cross-cultural communications.

Environmental Factors (Env)

1. Environmental performance index: Honduras scored 39.8 on the Environmental Performance Index, indicating significant environmental challenges and a need for better ecological management.

2. Carbon footprint per capita: The carbon footprint per capita in Honduras is relatively low, at around 1.3 metric tons of CO2, reflecting limited industrial activity.

3. Renewable energy usage percentage: Renewable energy constitutes about 60% of Honduras’ total energy consumption, with ongoing projects to increase this share, particularly in hydro and solar energy.
