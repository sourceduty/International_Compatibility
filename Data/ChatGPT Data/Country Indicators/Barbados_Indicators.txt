
Political Factors (P)

International Agreements Participation
- Barbados is involved in numerous international agreements, including recent cooperation agreements with Guyana and Suriname to enhance economic activities in areas such as agriculture, mining, tourism, and education. Additionally, Barbados has 31 Double Taxation Agreements (DTAs) and 11 Bilateral Investment Treaties (BITs) in force, which help promote foreign investment and economic cooperation.

Political Stability Index
- Barbados maintains a stable political environment with a strong democratic system. The country has managed to preserve macroeconomic stability despite global economic challenges, supported by comprehensive economic reform programs and resilient governance.

Corruption Perception Index
- According to Transparency International, Barbados has a relatively low level of corruption compared to other Caribbean nations. Recent efforts to enhance transparency and reduce corruption include regulatory frameworks to counter money laundering and terrorism financing.

Economic Factors (E)

GDP per Capita
- As of 2023, Barbados's GDP per capita is approximately $17,000. The economy is driven by tourism, financial services, and manufacturing sectors.

Trade Openness Index
- Barbados has a high trade openness index, facilitated by its participation in the World Trade Organization (WTO) and various trade agreements. The country actively seeks to enhance its trade relations and diversify its economic base.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows
- Barbados continues to attract substantial FDI, particularly in sectors like tourism, real estate, and financial services. The favorable investment climate and regulatory environment support these inflows.

Social Factors (S)

Education Index
- The education system in Barbados is well-developed, with high literacy rates and significant government investment in education at all levels. The country emphasizes quality education to support its economic and social development.

Healthcare Quality Index
- Barbados provides comprehensive and high-quality healthcare services. The healthcare infrastructure ensures accessible and effective care for all citizens, with ongoing investments to improve health outcomes.

Human Development Index (HDI)
- Barbados ranks high on the Human Development Index, reflecting strong performance in health, education, and income levels. Continued investments in social services are essential to maintaining and improving this high ranking.

Cultural Factors (C)

Cultural Proximity Index
- The cultural dimensions of Barbados, influenced by its British colonial history, reflect high individualism and low power distance. This cultural alignment supports social cohesion and international business.

Language Similarity Index
- The official language of Barbados is English, which facilitates communication and cultural exchange with other English-speaking countries. This linguistic similarity enhances international cooperation and business relationships.

Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability
- Barbados' multicultural society and robust education system contribute to strong adaptability in cross-cultural communication. The population's exposure to diverse cultures supports effective international interactions.
