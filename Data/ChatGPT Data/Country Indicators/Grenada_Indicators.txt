
Political Factors (P)

International agreements participation: Grenada is a member of multiple international organizations including the United Nations (UN), the Caribbean Community (CARICOM), and the Organization of Eastern Caribbean States (OECS). It participates in various international agreements related to trade, climate change, and regional cooperation (TravelTips.org).

2. Political stability index: The latest World Bank data indicates that Grenada has a relatively stable political environment, though specific numerical data might need to be accessed directly from the World Bank’s Governance Indicators (LloydsBankTrade.com).

3. Corruption perception index: According to Transparency International, Grenada’s Corruption Perception Index (CPI) score in recent years places it among countries with moderate levels of perceived corruption (TravelTips.org).

Economic Factors (E)

1. GDP per capita: As of the latest data, Grenada’s GDP per capita is approximately $10,041 USD, indicating a middle-income economy (LloydsBankTrade.com).

2. Trade openness index: Grenada’s economy is open, with foreign trade representing about 77% of its GDP. The country is heavily involved in trade, particularly within the CARICOM framework and with major partners such as the United States and Trinidad and Tobago (LloydsBankTrade.com).

3. Foreign direct investment (FDI) inflows: Grenada has seen varying levels of FDI, with significant investments in tourism, infrastructure, and services sectors. The World Bank and other sources provide detailed statistics on annual FDI inflows (IMF.org).

Social Factors (S)

1. Education index: Grenada has a relatively high literacy rate and a developing education system, supported by international cooperation with countries like Cuba for educational development (TravelTips.org).

2. Healthcare quality index: The healthcare system in Grenada benefits from partnerships with countries like Cuba, which provide medical aid and support. Health indicators are improving but detailed index values should be referred to in specific health reports (TravelTips.org).

3. Human development index (HDI): Grenada’s HDI is categorized as high, reflecting improvements in life expectancy, education, and per capita income levels (LloydsBankTrade.com).

Cultural Factors (C)

1. Cultural proximity index (e.g., Hofstede's cultural dimensions): Cultural indices specific to Grenada might not be readily available, but it shares many cultural traits common within the Caribbean region, influenced by colonial history and regional integration (TravelTips.org).

2. Language similarity index: English is the official language, and cultural exchange programs and international relations help foster communication adaptability (TravelTips.org).

3. Cross-cultural communication adaptability: Grenada’s participation in international and regional organizations enhances its adaptability in cross-cultural communications (TravelTips.org).

Environmental Factors (Env)

1. Environmental performance index: Grenada is actively engaged in climate change initiatives and environmental conservation efforts. Specific EPI scores can be found through environmental performance reports by organizations like Yale University and the United Nations (TravelTips.org).

2. Carbon footprint per capita: The carbon footprint is relatively low compared to larger nations, but efforts are ongoing to reduce emissions further through renewable energy projects (LloydsBankTrade.com).

3. Renewable energy usage percentage: Grenada is investing in renewable energy, with several projects aimed at increasing the share of renewables in its energy mix, supported by international aid and cooperation (LloydsBankTrade.com).
