
Political Factors (P)

International Agreements Participation
- Burkina Faso is engaged in various international agreements. It is a member of regional organizations such as the Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS) and participates in global agreements focusing on trade, security, and environmental sustainability. Recent participation includes the approval of a significant budget for the Priority Action Plan for Development (PASD), focusing on resilience, security, social cohesion, and economic growth.

Political Stability Index
- Burkina Faso has experienced political instability, particularly highlighted by a military coup in January 2022. The transitional government is in place, with plans to hold elections in July 2024. The political environment remains volatile due to ongoing security challenges posed by extremist groups and internal displacement.

Corruption Perception Index
- According to Transparency International, Burkina Faso ranks 77th out of 180 countries on the Corruption Perception Index, indicating moderate levels of corruption. Efforts to combat corruption are ongoing but face significant challenges.

Economic Factors (E)

GDP per Capita
- As of 2023, Burkina Faso's GDP per capita is approximately $830. The economy relies heavily on agriculture, mining (especially gold), and cotton production.

Trade Openness Index
- Burkina Faso has a moderate trade openness index, facilitated by its membership in ECOWAS and participation in various international trade fairs and exhibitions aimed at attracting foreign investment and promoting exports.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows
- Burkina Faso continues to attract foreign direct investment, particularly in the mining and agricultural sectors. The government hosts several trade fairs and exhibitions to boost FDI, despite challenges posed by political instability.

Social Factors (S)

Education Index
- Burkina Faso has made progress in education, with increased school enrollment rates. However, the overall quality of education remains low, and significant disparities exist between urban and rural areas.

Healthcare Quality Index
- The healthcare system in Burkina Faso faces significant challenges, including limited access to quality healthcare services, particularly in rural areas. Efforts are being made to improve healthcare infrastructure and services.

Human Development Index (HDI)
- Burkina Faso ranks low on the Human Development Index, reflecting challenges in health, education, and income levels. Continued efforts are needed to improve these indicators and address socio-economic disparities.

Cultural Factors (C)

Cultural Proximity Index
- Burkina Faso's cultural dimensions, influenced by its diverse ethnic groups and traditional values, reflect high power distance and collectivism. These cultural traits impact social interactions and organizational behavior within the country.

Language Similarity Index
- The official language of Burkina Faso is French, with numerous indigenous languages spoken across the country. This linguistic diversity facilitates communication within the region and enhances cross-cultural adaptability.

Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability
- Burkina Faso’s multicultural society and emphasis on education contribute to adaptability in cross-cultural communication. The population's exposure to diverse cultures supports effective international interactions.

Environmental Factors (Env)

Environmental Performance Index
- Burkina Faso faces significant environmental challenges, including deforestation and desertification. The government is working on policies to improve environmental sustainability and manage natural resources effectively.

Carbon Footprint per Capita
- Burkina Faso’s carbon footprint per capita is low, reflecting its developing economy and limited industrial activities. Efforts are being made to increase the use of renewable energy and promote environmental conservation.

Renewable Energy Usage Percentage
- The usage of renewable energy in Burkina Faso is growing, with investments in solar energy projects. These initiatives aim to diversify the energy mix and promote sustainability.
