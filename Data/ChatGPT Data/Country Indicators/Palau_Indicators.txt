
Political Factors (P)

- International agreements participation: Palau has actively participated in several international agreements, including the Compacts of Free Association with the United States and the recently ratified Marine Biodiversity Treaty under the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea.
- Political stability index: Palau maintains a stable political environment, although specific index values are not readily available. The country benefits from its strategic partnership with the United States.
- Corruption perception index: Palau's corruption perception index score and ranking can be accessed via Transparency International's latest reports.

Economic Factors (E)

- GDP per capita: According to the World Bank, Palau’s GDP per capita is approximately $14,727 (2021).
- Trade openness index: Palau's economy is highly open, relying significantly on imports and tourism.
- Foreign direct investment (FDI) inflows: Palau receives considerable FDI, particularly in the tourism and hospitality sectors, facilitated by its strategic agreements and political stability.

Social Factors (S)

- Education index: Palau has a relatively high literacy rate and a well-established education system, supported by international partnerships.
- Healthcare quality index: The healthcare system in Palau is robust, with significant investments and support from international agreements, though specific index values should be referenced from the World Health Organization or similar databases.
- Human development index (HDI): Palau’s HDI is 0.814, categorizing it in the high human development tier according to the UNDP (2021).

Cultural Factors (C)

- Cultural proximity index: Palau shares cultural ties with other Pacific Island nations and has significant influences from its historical ties with the United States and Japan.
- Language similarity index: Palauan and English are the official languages, facilitating ease of communication both locally and internationally.
- Cross-cultural communication adaptability: High adaptability due to the multicultural environment fostered by tourism and international partnerships.

Environmental Factors (Env)

- Environmental performance index: Palau performs well in environmental protection, with strong marine conservation efforts and participation in global environmental agreements.
- Carbon footprint per capita: Palau has a relatively low carbon footprint per capita compared to larger industrial nations.
- Renewable energy usage percentage: Palau is increasingly investing in renewable energy, with several initiatives to reduce dependence on fossil fuels and enhance sustainability.
