
Political Factors (P)

1. International Agreements Participation:
   Fiji is actively engaged in various international agreements, including the United Nations (UN), World Trade Organization (WTO), International Monetary Fund (IMF), and the Paris Agreement on climate change. Regionally, Fiji is part of the Pacific Islands Forum, the South Pacific Regional Environment Programme (SPREP), and the Melanesian Spearhead Group (MSG). These agreements highlight Fiji's commitment to global cooperation and environmental sustainability.

2. Political Stability Index:
   Fiji has experienced political instability in the past, including coups in 1987, 2000, and 2006. However, recent years have seen relative political stability, bolstered by democratic elections and reforms aimed at enhancing governance and political processes.

3. Corruption Perception Index:
   Transparency International ranks Fiji 45th out of 180 countries in its Corruption Perceptions Index, indicating a moderate level of perceived corruption. Efforts to combat corruption include legal reforms and the establishment of anti-corruption bodies.

Economic Factors (E)

1. GDP per Capita:
   As of 2022, Fiji's GDP per capita is approximately $12,096 USD. The economy is primarily driven by tourism, agriculture, and manufacturing sectors.

2. Trade Openness Index:
   Fiji has a high trade openness index, with major trading partners including Australia, New Zealand, China, and the United States. The country's strategic location in the Pacific facilitates significant trade activity, although it maintains a trade deficit due to higher imports than exports.

3. Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows:
   Fiji attracts FDI mainly in tourism, real estate, and manufacturing sectors. The government provides incentives to foreign investors to boost economic growth and diversify the economy.

Social Factors (S)

1. Education Index:
   Fiji invests significantly in education, with a literacy rate of around 94%. The education system includes free primary and secondary education, with ongoing efforts to improve the quality and accessibility of higher education.

2. Healthcare Quality Index:
   The healthcare system in Fiji provides basic services to the population, but it faces challenges such as limited resources and infrastructure. Healthcare spending is around 4% of GDP, and the country is working on improving healthcare delivery and access.

3. Human Development Index (HDI):
   Fiji's HDI is 0.743, placing it in the high human development category. This reflects significant progress in health, education, and income over recent years.

Cultural Factors (C)

1. Cultural Proximity Index:
   Fiji scores high in collectivism and power distance, reflecting a society that values family and community ties, as well as hierarchical structures. These cultural dimensions influence social interactions and governance.

2. Language Similarity Index:
   English is the official language of Fiji, along with Fijian and Hindi. This multilingual environment facilitates communication within the country and with international partners.

3. Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability:
   Fiji's diverse cultural heritage and emphasis on tourism have fostered strong cross-cultural communication skills, essential for its global interactions and international business engagements.

Environmental Factors (Env)

1. Environmental Performance Index:
   Fiji faces environmental challenges such as rising sea levels and natural disasters. The country is proactive in addressing these issues through various environmental policies and international agreements aimed at sustainability and climate resilience.

2. Carbon Footprint per Capita:
   Fiji's carbon footprint per capita is relatively low, reflecting its efforts to balance economic growth with environmental sustainability. The country focuses on renewable energy sources to reduce carbon emissions.

3. Renewable Energy Usage Percentage:
   Fiji is increasing its investment in renewable energy, particularly in hydropower and solar energy, which are becoming significant components of its energy mix. These efforts are part of Fiji's broader strategy to enhance energy security and sustainability.
