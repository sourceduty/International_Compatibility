
Political Factors (P)

1. International Agreements Participation:
   Finland is actively involved in numerous international agreements. It is a member of the United Nations (UN), World Trade Organization (WTO), International Monetary Fund (IMF), and NATO. Finland has also ratified environmental agreements such as the Paris Agreement and the Helsinki Convention on the protection of the Baltic Sea. These commitments highlight Finland's role in global cooperation and environmental protection.

2. Political Stability Index:
   Finland is known for its political stability, characterized by a robust democratic system and low levels of political unrest. The country’s political environment is strengthened by its EU membership and adherence to international laws and agreements.

3. Corruption Perception Index:
   Finland ranks highly on Transparency International's Corruption Perceptions Index, indicating low levels of corruption. The country is recognized for its strong governance and transparency in public administration.

Economic Factors (E)

1. GDP per Capita:
   As of the latest data, Finland's GDP per capita is approximately $56,000 USD, reflecting a well-developed economy driven by sectors such as information technology, manufacturing, and services.

2. Trade Openness Index:
   Finland has a high trade openness index, facilitated by its membership in the European Union and active participation in global trade agreements through the WTO. The country maintains significant trade relationships with Germany, Sweden, and Russia.

3. Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows:
   Finland attracts substantial FDI, particularly in technology, energy, and manufacturing sectors. The government supports foreign investment through favorable policies and a stable economic environment.

Social Factors (S)

1. Education Index:
   Finland is renowned for its education system, which emphasizes high quality and accessibility. The country invests significantly in education, resulting in high literacy rates and strong educational outcomes.

2. Healthcare Quality Index:
   Finland has a comprehensive healthcare system, funded by public and private sectors. The country ranks highly in healthcare quality, with significant investments in medical infrastructure and services.

3. Human Development Index (HDI):
   Finland's HDI is 0.940, placing it among the highest in the world. This reflects excellent performance in health, education, and income, contributing to the overall well-being of its citizens.

Cultural Factors (C)

1. Cultural Proximity Index:
   Using Hofstede's cultural dimensions, Finland exhibits low power distance and high individualism, indicating a society that values equality and personal freedom. These cultural traits influence social and organizational behavior.

2. Language Similarity Index:
   Finnish and Swedish are the official languages, with English widely spoken. This multilingual environment facilitates regional and international communication.

3. Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability:
   Finland’s diverse cultural interactions, driven by international trade and tourism, enhance its adaptability in cross-cultural communication, crucial for global business engagements.

Environmental Factors (Env)

1. Environmental Performance Index:
   Finland is a global leader in environmental performance, with strong policies on sustainable development and environmental protection. The country has significantly reduced pollution and promotes conservation efforts.

2. Carbon Footprint per Capita:
   Finland's carbon footprint per capita is relatively high due to its energy-intensive industries and cold climate, but the country is making efforts to reduce emissions through renewable energy and efficiency improvements.

3. Renewable Energy Usage Percentage:
   Approximately 25% of Finland's energy comes from renewable sources, including biomass, wind, and hydroelectric power. The country continues to invest in expanding its renewable energy capacity.
