
Political Factors (P)

International Agreements Participation:
Liechtenstein is a member of several key international agreements including the European Economic Area (EEA) Agreement, the Hague Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Decisions, and the Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards (New York Convention).

Political Stability Index:
Liechtenstein is known for its political stability, ranking high in global indexes. The latest Political Stability and Absence of Violence/Terrorism indicator from the World Bank places Liechtenstein in the top percentile globally.

Corruption Perception Index:
According to Transparency International's 2022 Corruption Perceptions Index, Liechtenstein scores 82 out of 100, ranking it among the least corrupt countries in the world.

Economic Factors (E)

GDP per Capita:
The World Bank reports that Liechtenstein has a very high GDP per capita, exceeding $180,000 as of the latest data, reflecting its affluent economy.

Trade Openness Index:
Liechtenstein is highly integrated into the global economy, with a trade openness index score of 88.5 according to the Heritage Foundation's 2023 Index of Economic Freedom.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows:
UNCTAD's World Investment Report indicates that Liechtenstein attracts significant FDI, primarily due to its favorable tax regime and business-friendly environment.

Social Factors (S)

Education Index:
Liechtenstein has a high education index, with near-universal literacy rates and strong educational outcomes, as reported by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP).

Healthcare Quality Index:
The country boasts a robust healthcare system, consistently ranking high on the Healthcare Access and Quality Index by The Lancet.

Human Development Index (HDI):
Liechtenstein has an HDI of 0.954, placing it among the highest in the world according to the latest UNDP Human Development Report.

Cultural Factors (C)

Cultural Proximity Index:
Liechtenstein's cultural dimensions align closely with neighboring German-speaking countries, scoring high on individualism and low on power distance in Hofstede's framework.

Language Similarity Index:
The primary language is German, closely related to the languages spoken in neighboring Switzerland and Austria, facilitating high language similarity.

Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability:
Liechtenstein's population is known for its multilingual abilities and adaptability in cross-cultural communication, owing to its small size and high degree of international interaction.

Environmental Factors (Env)

Environmental Performance Index:
The 2022 Environmental Performance Index ranks Liechtenstein high, particularly in categories like air quality and biodiversity.

Carbon Footprint per Capita:
Liechtenstein has a relatively high carbon footprint per capita due to its affluent lifestyle, but efforts are being made to reduce emissions through renewable energy initiatives.
