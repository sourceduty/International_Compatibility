
Political Factors (P)

International Agreements Participation
- Belize is actively involved in various international agreements, including trade agreements with the Caribbean Community (CARICOM), the Caribbean Basin Initiative (CBI), and the CARIBCAN agreement with Canada. It also has a Partial Scope Agreement with Taiwan and is part of the U.S. Generalized System of Preferences (GSP) and the U.S.-Caribbean Basin Trade Partnership Act (CBTPA).

Political Stability Index
- Belize maintains a relatively stable political environment, though it faces challenges such as addressing official complicity in trafficking crimes and managing migration flows. The government is working on improving political stability through various reforms and international cooperation.

Corruption Perception Index
- Transparency International ranks Belize moderately in terms of corruption, indicating ongoing challenges with governance and corruption. Efforts to combat corruption are ongoing, but more effective enforcement is needed.

Economic Factors (E)

GDP per Capita
- As of 2023, Belize's GDP per capita is approximately $5,300. The economy is primarily driven by tourism, agriculture, and services sectors.

Trade Openness Index
- Belize has a high trade openness index, facilitated by its participation in various trade agreements and its strategic efforts to enhance trade relations with countries like Mexico and Taiwan.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows
- Belize continues to attract foreign direct investment, particularly in tourism, real estate, and agriculture. Efforts to improve the investment climate and regulatory framework are ongoing to boost FDI inflows.

Social Factors (S)

Education Index
- Belize has a high literacy rate and a well-developed education system, with significant government investment in education at all levels. The focus is on improving educational outcomes and accessibility.

Healthcare Quality Index
- The healthcare system in Belize provides comprehensive medical services, with ongoing investments to enhance healthcare infrastructure and services. Efforts are being made to ensure equitable access to quality healthcare for all citizens.

Human Development Index (HDI)
- Belize ranks moderately on the Human Development Index, reflecting progress in health, education, and living standards. Continued investments in social services are necessary to improve its HDI ranking further.

Cultural Factors (C)

Cultural Proximity Index
- Belize's cultural dimensions, influenced by its diverse population and British colonial history, reflect a society with moderate individualism and low power distance. These cultural traits impact social interactions and organizational behavior.

Language Similarity Index
- The official language of Belize is English, which facilitates communication and cultural exchange with other English-speaking countries. This linguistic similarity enhances international cooperation and business relationships.

Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability
- Belize's multicultural society and robust education system contribute to strong adaptability in cross-cultural communication. The population's exposure to diverse cultures supports effective international interactions.
