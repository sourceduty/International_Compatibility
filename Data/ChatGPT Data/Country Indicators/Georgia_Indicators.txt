
### Political Factors (P)
1. International Agreements Participation:
   - Georgia is a member of multiple international organizations, including the United Nations, World Trade Organization, and Council of Europe. It has signed various bilateral and multilateral agreements, particularly focused on trade and security.

2. Political Stability Index:
   - The latest value for Georgia's political stability index is -0.44 (2022) on a scale from -2.5 (weak) to 2.5 (strong). The country has shown improvements over the years, reflecting a relatively stable political environment compared to its past.

3. Corruption Perception Index:
   - In 2023, Georgia scored 56 on the Corruption Perceptions Index, indicating a moderate level of perceived corruption. This score reflects ongoing efforts to combat corruption and improve governance.

### Economic Factors (E)
1. GDP per Capita:
   - As of 2022, Georgia's GDP per capita is approximately $6,073. This figure represents the average income per person and provides an overview of the country's economic performance.

2. Trade Openness Index:
   - Georgia maintains a high trade openness index, largely due to its strategic location and free trade agreements with the European Union and other countries. This index measures the ratio of trade (exports + imports) to GDP, emphasizing Georgia's integration into the global economy.

3. Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows:
   - In recent years, Georgia has attracted significant FDI, with inflows reaching around $1.24 billion in 2022. These investments are crucial for economic growth and development.

### Social Factors (S)
1. Education Index:
   - Georgia's education index is relatively high, with a strong emphasis on improving literacy rates and access to higher education. The index measures the average years of schooling for adults and expected years of schooling for children.

2. Healthcare Quality Index:
   - The healthcare quality index for Georgia shows progress in improving healthcare services, although challenges remain. The index considers various factors, including life expectancy, healthcare infrastructure, and access to medical services.

3. Human Development Index (HDI):
   - Georgia's HDI value for 2022 is 0.786, placing it in the high human development category. This composite index measures average achievement in key dimensions of human development: a long and healthy life, knowledge, and a decent standard of living.

### Cultural Factors (C)
1. Cultural Proximity Index:
   - Based on Hofstede's cultural dimensions, Georgia exhibits characteristics such as high power distance and collectivism. These cultural traits influence business practices and social interactions within the country.

2. Language Similarity Index:
   - The primary language in Georgia is Georgian, with a high degree of language uniformity. However, Russian and English are also commonly spoken, especially in business and tourism sectors.

3. Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability:
   - Georgia has a moderate level of adaptability in cross-cultural communication, with increasing exposure to international influences through tourism and business interactions.

### Environmental Factors (Env)
1. Environmental Performance Index:
   - Georgia's environmental performance index score is 53.4, indicating moderate performance in terms of sustainability and environmental policies. This index assesses various factors such as air quality, biodiversity, and climate change policies.

2. Carbon Footprint per Capita:
   - The carbon footprint per capita in Georgia is relatively low compared to global averages, reflecting lower industrial activity and energy consumption levels.

3. Renewable Energy Usage Percentage:
   - Approximately 30% of Georgia's energy comes from renewable sources, primarily hydroelectric power. This percentage highlights the country's commitment to sustainable energy practices and reducing reliance on fossil fuels.
