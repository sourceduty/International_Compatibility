
Political Factors (P)

- International Agreements Participation: Nicaragua is a participant in various international agreements, including the Central America-Dominican Republic Free Trade Agreement (CAFTA-DR) and agreements with Russia for peaceful use of nuclear energy.
- Political Stability Index: Nicaragua has faced significant political instability, with reports of systematic human rights violations, suppression of political opposition, and severe restrictions on freedoms of expression and association.
- Corruption Perception Index: Transparency International ranked Nicaragua 159th out of 180 countries in its 2022 Corruption Perception Index, indicating high levels of perceived corruption.

Economic Factors (E)

- GDP per Capita: As of the latest data, Nicaragua's GDP per capita is approximately $2,200 USD.
- Trade Openness Index: Nicaragua is engaged in several trade agreements, including CAFTA-DR, which facilitate trade openness.
- Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows: FDI inflows into Nicaragua have been fluctuating due to political instability and economic challenges. Recent agreements with countries like Russia indicate efforts to attract more FDI.

Social Factors (S)

- Education Index: Nicaragua's education index reflects challenges in educational quality and access, significantly impacted by the political and economic climate.
- Healthcare Quality Index: Healthcare quality in Nicaragua is relatively low, with significant disparities in access and services, especially in rural areas.
- Human Development Index (HDI): Nicaragua's HDI is 0.667, placing it in the medium human development category, with significant room for improvement in health, education, and income.

Cultural Factors (C)

- Cultural Proximity Index: Using Hofstede's cultural dimensions, Nicaragua scores high on power distance and collectivism, reflecting a hierarchical society with strong family ties.
- Language Similarity Index: Spanish is the official language of Nicaragua, creating cultural and linguistic proximity with other Spanish-speaking countries in Latin America.
- Cross-cultural Communication Adaptability: Nicaraguans generally exhibit adaptability in cross-cultural communication, facilitated by cultural values of collectivism and high-context communication styles.

Environmental Factors (Env)

- Environmental Performance Index: Nicaragua ranks low on the Environmental Performance Index, indicating significant challenges in environmental protection and sustainability efforts.
- Carbon Footprint per Capita: Nicaragua's carbon footprint per capita is relatively low due to its lower industrial activity but is rising with increasing energy consumption.
- Renewable Energy Usage Percentage: Nicaragua has made strides in renewable energy, with a significant percentage of its energy coming from renewable sources, particularly geothermal and wind.
