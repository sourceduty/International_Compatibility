Economic Factors (E)

GDP per Capita
- As of 2023, the GDP per capita of the Bahamas is approximately $33,000. The economy is primarily driven by tourism, financial services, and real estate, which contribute significantly to its GDP.

Trade Openness Index
- The Bahamas has a high trade openness index, facilitated by its strategic location and active participation in international trade agreements. The United States is the main trading partner, accounting for a significant portion of imports and exports.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows
- The Bahamas continues to attract substantial FDI, especially in tourism, real estate, and financial services. The stable investment climate and favorable business environment contribute to its attractiveness for foreign investors.

Social Factors (S)

Education Index
- The education system in the Bahamas is well-developed, with high literacy rates and significant government investment in primary and tertiary education. The country emphasizes quality education to support its economic and social development.

Healthcare Quality Index
- The healthcare system in the Bahamas is comprehensive, providing high-quality medical services. Efforts are ongoing to improve healthcare infrastructure and accessibility, especially in the outer islands.

Human Development Index (HDI)
- The Bahamas ranks high on the Human Development Index, reflecting strong performance in health, education, and income levels. Continuous investments in social services are essential to maintaining this high ranking.

Cultural Factors (C)

Cultural Proximity Index
- The cultural dimensions of the Bahamas, influenced by its British colonial history, reflect high individualism and low power distance, similar to other Western nations. This cultural alignment supports social cohesion and international business.

Language Similarity Index
- The official language of the Bahamas is English, which facilitates communication and cultural exchange with other English-speaking countries. This linguistic similarity enhances international cooperation and business relationships.

Cross-Cultural Communication Adaptability
- The Bahamas' multicultural society and robust education system contribute to strong adaptability in cross-cultural communication. The population's exposure to diverse cultures supports effective international interactions.

Environmental Factors (Env)

Environmental Performance Index
- The Bahamas faces environmental challenges, including vulnerability to climate change and natural disasters. The government is actively working on policies to improve environmental sustainability and resilience.

Carbon Footprint per Capita
- The carbon footprint per capita in the Bahamas is moderate, with ongoing efforts to reduce greenhouse gas emissions through increased use of renewable energy and energy efficiency measures.

Renewable Energy Usage Percentage
- The usage of renewable energy in the Bahamas is increasing, with significant investments in solar and wind energy. These initiatives are part of the broader strategy to promote sustainability and reduce reliance on fossil fuels.
