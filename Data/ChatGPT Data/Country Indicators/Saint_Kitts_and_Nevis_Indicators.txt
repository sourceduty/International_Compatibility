
Political Factors (P)

- International Agreements Participation:
  Saint Kitts and Nevis is an active participant in several international agreements. The country is involved in the United Nations, the Commonwealth, the Organization of American States (OAS), and the Caribbean Community (CARICOM). It has also signed various bilateral agreements, including recent partnerships with São Tomé and Príncipe, Serbia, Palestine, Burkina Faso, and Gabon.

- Political Stability Index:
  Saint Kitts and Nevis enjoys a relatively stable political environment, with a comprehensive crime management strategy and initiatives to improve governance and transparency contributing to this stability.

- Corruption Perception Index:
  Saint Kitts and Nevis is working on improving governance and reducing corruption. Although specific recent rankings were not detailed, the country has made efforts to enhance integrity in public life and establish anti-corruption measures.

Economic Factors (E)

- GDP per Capita:
  As of the latest data, Saint Kitts and Nevis' GDP per capita is approximately $17,090 (World Bank, 2022).

- Trade Openness Index:
  The country has a high trade openness index due to its extensive trade relations within CARICOM and with major global partners, reflecting significant import and export activities.

- Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Inflows:
  In 2022, Saint Kitts and Nevis attracted significant FDI, driven by investments in tourism, health tourism, and modern manufacturing industries, totaling over $100 million, with more projects in the pipeline.

Social Factors (S)

- Education Index:
  Saint Kitts and Nevis has made significant strides in education, offering free tuition at the community college level and reducing university costs, resulting in a moderate to high education index.

- Healthcare Quality Index:
  The healthcare system is focused on improving access and quality through initiatives like national health insurance and climate-smart infrastructure. The healthcare quality index is moderate, reflecting ongoing improvements.

- Human Development Index (HDI):
  Saint Kitts and Nevis ranks high in human development, with a focus on improving health, education, and social protection. The HDI for 2022 was 0.779 (UNDP).

Cultural Factors (C)

- Cultural Proximity Index:
  Saint Kitts and Nevis exhibits cultural traits common to the Caribbean, with high collectivism and power distance, indicating strong community ties and hierarchical structures.

- Language Similarity Index:
  English is the official language, facilitating communication within the region and with global partners.

- Cross-cultural Communication Adaptability:
  The population shows high adaptability in cross-cultural communication, influenced by the diverse cultural interactions within the Caribbean and international diplomatic engagements.

Environmental Factors (Env)

- Environmental Performance Index:
  Saint Kitts and Nevis scores moderately on the Environmental Performance Index, with significant efforts in climate change mitigation and environmental sustainability.

- Carbon Footprint per Capita:
  The carbon footprint per capita is relatively low at approximately 3.8 metric tons of CO2, reflecting its small industrial base and reliance on renewable energy sources.

- Renewable Energy Usage Percentage:
  Around 30% of the country's energy consumption comes from renewable sources, primarily solar and wind energy, highlighting a commitment to sustainable energy practices.
